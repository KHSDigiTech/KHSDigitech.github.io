üé® AS91893 - Digital Media Outcome

Use advanced processes to develop a digital media outcome

AS91893 ‚Ä¢ 4 Credits ‚Ä¢ Internal
Grade Level Criteria & Evidence Requirements
Achieved
  • Follows a structured design process with clear planning documentation
  • Engages with stakeholders to gather requirements and feedback
  • Creates a complete, functional digital media outcome (game)
  • Uses advanced tools and techniques appropriately
  • Documents the development process and design decisions
  • Shows evidence of testing and basic improvements
Evidence Examples:
  • Game Design Document with clear objectives and planning
  • Stakeholder feedback sessions (minimum 2-3 different people)
  • Working game with complete gameplay loop
  • Development journal showing process
  • Basic user testing with documented improvements
Merit
  • All Achieved criteria PLUS:
  • Demonstrates informed decision-making based on stakeholder needs
  • Shows clear understanding of design principles and user experience
  • Integrates feedback effectively to improve the outcome
  • Uses advanced techniques with clear understanding and rationale
  • Outcome shows sophistication and attention to detail
Evidence Examples:
  • Detailed analysis of user needs and how they influenced design
  • Multiple feedback sessions with comprehensive responses
  • Clear before/after examples showing iterative improvement
  • Sophisticated use of design principles (color theory, UI/UX)
  • Well-justified technical and artistic choices
Excellence
  • All Achieved and Merit criteria PLUS:
  • Demonstrates comprehensive understanding of advanced design concepts
  • Shows extensive iterative refinement based on thorough testing
  • Outcome is polished, professional-quality, and highly effective
  • Advanced techniques are used innovatively and effectively
  • Deep integration of stakeholder feedback with sophisticated analysis
Evidence Examples:
  • Extensive user testing with diverse stakeholder groups
  • Professional-level polish in visual design and user experience
  • Innovative or particularly sophisticated design solutions
  • Comprehensive analysis of design effectiveness
  • Exceptional integration of feedback into refined outcome

üí° Assessment Tips

Maximizing Your Grade

  • Document Everything: Keep detailed records of your process from day one
  • Seek Feedback Early: Start user testing in Sprint 1, not Sprint 4
  • Show Your Thinking: Explain why you made specific decisions
  • Quality Over Quantity: Better to have fewer features that work excellently
  • Test on Others: Your game must work for people who didn't create it
  • Reflect Deeply: Show learning and growth throughout the process

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Leaving documentation until the end - it should be ongoing
  • Not engaging with real stakeholders - friends and family count!
  • Focusing only on technical implementation without design consideration
  • Creating a game that only you can understand or play
  • Not showing evidence of iteration and improvement
  • Submitting work that doesn't run or has critical bugs
