AS 92007 Design Portfolio Task 2025

Design a Digital Technologies Outcome - Level 1, 5 Credits

🎯 Task Overview

You are designing a comprehensive digital solution to address a real-world problem affecting young people aged 16-25. This could be a mobile app, website, game, or software application that makes a meaningful difference in their lives.

Your Challenge

Create a portfolio of design work that documents your journey from identifying a problem to designing a complete digital solution. Focus on how your design thinking evolves, how you use feedback, and how your final design serves its intended purpose.

Achievement

  • Design a digital technologies outcome
  • Describe need/opportunity and users
  • Generate design ideas
  • Show how design addresses the need

Merit

  • Refine a design for a digital technologies outcome
  • Use feedback to improve design
  • Explain how decisions improve quality

Excellence

  • Evaluate a design for a digital technologies outcome
  • Justify how decisions contribute to fitness for purpose
  • Show deep understanding of design impact

📋 Portfolio Development Checklist

1. Problem Identification & User Research
Identify a genuine need or opportunity
Choose a real problem that affects young people (16-25) in areas like study, work, health, social connection, or personal development.

Sentence Starters:

  • "The problem I identified is..."
  • "This issue particularly affects young people because..."
  • "Currently, this need is not well addressed because..."
Define potential users and their specific requirements
Create detailed user profiles including their needs, limitations, preferences, and technical abilities.

Sentence Starters:

  • "My primary users are..."
  • "Their main requirements include..."
  • "They need this solution to be... because..."
  • "The key challenges they face are..."
Gather research evidence to support your problem
Include surveys, interviews, statistics, or observations that prove this is a genuine issue.

Sentence Starters:

  • "My research shows that..."
  • "I discovered through [method] that..."
  • "The data indicates..."
2. Design Ideas & Initial Concepts
Generate multiple design ideas
Create at least 3-5 different approaches to solving your identified problem. Show visual elements, functional features, and technical considerations.

Sentence Starters:

  • "My first design idea focuses on..."
  • "An alternative approach would be to..."
  • "This concept addresses the problem by..."
  • "The key features of this design include..."
Create sketches, wireframes, or concept diagrams
Show your ideas visually through hand-drawn sketches, digital wireframes, or system diagrams.
Explain how each idea addresses user needs
Connect your design features back to the specific requirements you identified.

Sentence Starters:

  • "This design element helps users by..."
  • "I included this feature because my research showed..."
  • "This addresses the requirement for... by..."
3. Manaakitanga & Kaitiakitanga Integration
Show how you applied manaakitanga (care and respect for others)
Demonstrate how your design considers accessibility, inclusivity, cultural sensitivity, and user wellbeing.

Sentence Starters:

  • "I applied manaakitanga by ensuring..."
  • "To show care for all users, I..."
  • "My design is inclusive because..."
  • "I considered different cultural perspectives by..."
Demonstrate kaitiakitanga (environmental and digital responsibility)
Show how your design considers data privacy, digital wellbeing, sustainable technology use, or environmental impact.

Sentence Starters:

  • "I demonstrated kaitiakitanga through..."
  • "To protect user data and privacy, I designed..."
  • "My solution promotes healthy digital habits by..."
  • "I considered the environmental impact by..."
4. Feedback & Refinement Process
Collect feedback from potential users or experts
Get specific, actionable feedback on your design ideas from people who represent your target users.

Sentence Starters:

  • "When I showed my design to [user type], they said..."
  • "The feedback I received was..."
  • "Users particularly liked/disliked..."
Document specific changes made based on feedback
Show before/after comparisons and explain exactly what you changed and why.

Sentence Starters:

  • "Based on this feedback, I changed..."
  • "To address the concern about [issue], I..."
  • "This modification improves the design because..."
  • "The updated version now..."
Explain how changes improve the design quality
Connect your refinements to better user experience, functionality, or fitness for purpose.

Sentence Starters:

  • "This change improves the user experience by..."
  • "The design is now more effective because..."
  • "This refinement better addresses the original problem by..."
5. Final Design & Evaluation
Complete detailed final design
Create a comprehensive design showing all key screens/pages, features, and user interactions.
Justify key design decisions
Explain why you made specific choices about layout, features, colors, navigation, etc.

Sentence Starters:

  • "I chose [design element] because..."
  • "This decision was based on..."
  • "The [feature/design] is effective for my users because..."
  • "Through the design process, I learned that..."
Evaluate fitness for purpose
Analyze how well your final design solves the original problem and meets user requirements.

Sentence Starters:

  • "My design is fit for purpose because..."
  • "It successfully addresses the original problem by..."
  • "Users will benefit from this design because..."
  • "The key strengths of my final design are..."
  • "If I were to develop this further, I would..."
6. Portfolio Presentation & Requirements
Include minimum 3 required images
• Final design (multiple images if multi-page)
• Design ideas/concepts
• Examples of design refinement
Stay within 1000-word limit
Keep written explanations concise and focused. Use visuals to communicate where possible.
Format correctly for submission
8-10 A4 pages OR 8-10 slides OR 6 A3 pages, saved as PDF, readable black text, minimum 11pt font.

  • Websites (community, education, services)
  • Software applications (desktop tools)

  • Interactive experiences or animations
  • IoT/robotics projects

Not suitable: Simple logos, posters, or basic static designs

  • Mental health and wellbeing
  • Social connection and loneliness
  • Financial literacy and budgeting
  • Environmental awareness
  • Career planning and skill development
  • Health and fitness motivation
  • Cultural connection and identity

🎯 Evidence to Include

  • Research notes and user insights
  • Sketches and initial concepts

  • Feedback documentation
  • Final design mockups/wireframes
  • Reflective annotations

⚠️ Important Restrictions

NO AI tools allowed: You cannot use ChatGPT, Claude, or other AI tools to write any part of your portfolio text. All written responses must be your own work.

Portfolio work only: Focus on the design phase only - do not include any actual development/coding work.

💡 Success Tips

  • Start with real research: Talk to actual people who have the problem you're trying to solve
  • Show your thinking process: Document how your ideas evolve and why you make changes
  • Get meaningful feedback: Ask specific questions like "What would prevent you from using this?" rather than "Do you like it?"
  • Connect everything back to users: Always explain how your design choices benefit your target users
  • Use visuals effectively: Screenshots, diagrams, and sketches often communicate better than text
  • Be specific: Instead of "users want it to be easy," say "users need to complete the main task in under 3 steps"
