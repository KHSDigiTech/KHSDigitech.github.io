üì∏ Evidence You Should Extract from Your Game Design

üéØFinal Game Design Concept

Use your completed design document to show your final game concept and design decisions.

Extract from your design document:

  • Final game concept description
  • Visual mockups or sketches you created
  • UI/menu design ideas
  • Game mechanics and rules
  • Art style and aesthetic choices

How to describe it:

  • "My final game design addresses the delivery theme by..."
  • "The main gameplay involves..."
  • "Players would interact with the game by..."
  • "I designed the game to be..."

üë•Target Users & Their Needs

Identify who your game was designed for and what problem it solves.

From the Kiwinko example:

"My potential players are mainly 14-18 year old high school students... I will be participating in a national game jam where other contributors will give me feedback."

How to describe your users:

  • "My game targets... because..."
  • "These users need entertainment that is..."
  • "The problem my game solves is..."
  • "My users want games that are..."

‚úèÔ∏èDesign Process Documentation

Look through your design document and extract your initial concepts and iterations.

Find evidence of:

  • Your initial game concepts (like the 3 ideas in Kiwinko example)
  • Sketches and wireframes you created
  • Art style decisions and asset choices
  • UI/UX design iterations
  • Game mechanics evolution

üí¨Feedback & Iteration Evidence

Document the feedback you received and how you acted on it.

From the Kiwinko example:

"Jake wasn't sold on the idea... Adam and John both liked the idea and said it could be exciting if done well. Maybe by adding anti aircraft stuff to dodge..."

How to document feedback:

  • "When I showed my design to [person], they suggested..."
  • "Based on this feedback, I changed..."
  • "This improvement made the game better because..."
  • "The updated version now..."

ü§ùManaakitanga Evidence

Show how your design considers care and respect for users.

From the Kiwinko example:

"I will make sure that my graphics are High Contrasting, so that the user can see what is going on... visual clarity is important."

How to show manaakitanga:

  • "I designed my game to be accessible by..."
  • "To show care for all players, I..."
  • "My design considers different abilities by..."
  • "I made sure the game was inclusive by..."

üèÜDesign Decisions & Justification

Explain WHY you made specific design choices and how they improve the game.

From the Kiwinko example:

"I chose this idea because Pachinko style games have been getting more popular lately... This is also an opportunity to tap into."

How to justify decisions:

  • "I chose [design element] because..."
  • "This decision makes the game better for users by..."
  • "My design is fit for purpose because..."
  • "The key strengths of my design are..."
